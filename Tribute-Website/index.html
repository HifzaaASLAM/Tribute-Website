Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah

1876-1949

Introduction

Welcome to the tribute website dedicated to Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah, the founder of Pakistan. As one of the most prominent leaders in the history of South Asia, Jinnah played a pivotal role in the creation of Pakistan and remains an inspirational figure for millions around the world.Jinnah's political journey began in the early 20th century when he joined the Indian National Congress, advocating for Hindu-Muslim unity and constitutional reforms within the British Raj. However, disillusioned by the Congress' indifference to Muslim concerns, he later became a prominent leader of the All-India Muslim League.

Biography

  • December 25, 1876, in Karachi, British India.
  • Completed early education in Karachi and Bombay. Studied law at Lincoln's Inn, London.
  • Qualified as a barrister and practiced law in Bombay, gaining recognition for his integrity and commitment to justice.
